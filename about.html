What is electronics and communication engineering?

Electronics and communication engineering (ECE) is a discipline of engineering that involves developing and testing electronic circuits and communication devices like transmitters, receivers and integrated circuits. Engineers who specialise in this domain often need to utilise the principles of digital and analogue transmission and reception of signals. This field of engineering also involves working with microprocessors, solid-state devices, satellite communication tools, circuits and electronic devices.

What does an electronics & communication engineer do?

Electronics and communication engineer use their knowledge, creativity and analytical skills to design, develop and oversee the production of electronic and communication tools and systems. Here are a few activities an electronics and communication engineer perform: Do research, design and develop electronic equipment used in different systems Look after the manufacturing of communication and broadcast systems Conceptualise and design electronic equipment like radio, television, computer Develop control and communications systems﻿ Write usage recommendations Test designs in order to ensure efficiency, cost-effectiveness and safety of new prototypes Test and evaluate electronic and communication products Plan and execute electronics and communication projects ﻿

Electronics and communication engineer salary

The average annual salary for electronics and communication engineers in India is ₹2,42,729. However, your actual salary will depend on your experience, job location and other factors. Senior electronics and communication engineers earn more than entry-level employees.

What are the top employment areas for electronics and communication engineers?

Here are a few sectors where an electronics and communication engineer can find work opportunities:

Telecom sector

In this field of engineering, you will learn how to design and develop telecom equipment and manage and transmit telecom data through different modes of communication. This helps you get a job in the telecom sector.

IT sector

You should have coding skills to get a job in the IT sector. In electronics and communication engineering, you will learn the basics of computer science and programming languages. Thus, you can easily find a job in the IT sector.

Machine learning and robotics

You will study the basics of machine learning and artificial intelligence (AI) as part of your engineering curriculum. With that knowledge, you can easily find a job in the machine learning and robotics sector. An ECE professional with an interest in machine learning mainly does research work.
